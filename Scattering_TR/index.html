Finite Potential Barrier

What is calculated?

This plot shows the probability of a particle transmitting ($T$) or reflecting ($R$) when it encounters a rectangular energy barrier of height $V_0$. The x-axis represents the incident energy $E$ normalized by the barrier height ($\epsilon = E/V_0$).

How to interpret:

  • $\epsilon < 1$ (Quantum Tunneling): Classically, a particle with energy less than the barrier height would be 100% reflected. Quantum mechanics allows a non-zero probability for the particle to tunnel through. Notice $T > 0$ in this region!
  • $\epsilon > 1$ (Over-barrier Reflection): Sudden changes in potential at the barrier edges cause wave reflection even when the particle has enough energy to pass classically.
  • Resonances: Observe the peaks where $T=1$ for $\epsilon > 1$. These occur when the barrier width matches an integer multiple of half the particle's wavelength.

Finite Potential Well

What is calculated?

This plot displays the transmission ($T$) and reflection ($R$) probabilities for a particle passing over a rectangular potential well of depth $-V_0$. The x-axis is the normalized incident energy $\epsilon = E/V_0$.

How to interpret:

  • Scattering at $E > 0$: The abrupt drop and rise in potential scatter the wave function even if the particle is "above" the well.
  • Transmission Resonances: Periodic oscillations where $T=1$ occur when reflected waves from the front and back edges cancel perfectly (the Ramsauer-Townsend effect).
