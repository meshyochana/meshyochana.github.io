1. The Associated Legendre ODE Theory
The general (associated) Legendre equation arises in separation of variables for Laplace's equation in spherical coordinates, with $x = \cos\theta \in [-1,1]$.
$$(1-x^2)\frac{d^2 y}{dx^2} - 2x\frac{dy}{dx} + \left[l(l+1) - \frac{m^2}{1-x^2}\right]y = 0$$

Or in an equivalent form: $\;\dfrac{d}{dx}\!\left[(1-x^2)\dfrac{dy}{dx}\right] + \left[l(l+1) - \dfrac{m^2}{1-x^2}\right]y = 0$.

General Solution: $\;y(x) = c_1 P_l^m(x) + c_2 Q_l^m(x)$

The equation has two linearly independent solutions for each pair $(l,m)$. The parameter $l$ must be a non-negative integer for the solutions to remain finite on $[-1,1]$. The parameter $m$ is the order of the associated polynomial.

▶ First kind: Plm(x) — General form

$$P_l^m(x) = \frac{(-1)^m}{2^l\,l!}(1-x^2)^{m/2}\frac{d^{l+m}}{dx^{l+m}}(x^2-1)^l$$

Rodrigues formula for $m=0$: $\;P_l(x) = \dfrac{1}{2^l l!}\dfrac{d^l}{dx^l}(x^2-1)^l$.   Recurrence: $(l-m+1)P_{l+1}^m = (2l+1)x\,P_l^m - (l+m)P_{l-1}^m$

▶ Second kind: Qlm(x) — general form

$$Q_l^m(x) = (-1)^m(1-x^2)^{m/2}\frac{d^m}{dx^m}Q_l(x)$$ where $\;Q_l(x) = \frac{1}{2}P_l(x)\ln\!\frac{1+x}{1-x} - W_{l-1}(x)$

$Q_l$ is singular at $x = \pm 1$ and is excluded from physical applications requiring finite boundary conditions. See the DLMF Section 14.7 for further details.

Key properties of $P_l^m(x)$:
  • Orthogonality: $\displaystyle\int_{-1}^{1} P_l^m(x)\,P_{l'}^m(x)\,dx = \frac{2}{2l+1}\frac{(l+m)!}{(l-m)!}\,\delta_{ll'}$
  • Symmetry: $P_l^{-m}(x) = (-1)^m\dfrac{(l-m)!}{(l+m)!}P_l^m(x)$
  • Connection to spherical harmonics: $Y_l^m(\theta,\phi) = \sqrt{\frac{2l+1}{4\pi}\frac{(l-m)!}{(l+m)!}}\,P_l^m(\cos\theta)\,e^{im\phi}$
