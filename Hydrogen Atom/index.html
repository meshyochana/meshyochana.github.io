Visualise the hydrogen wavefunction $\psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\varphi)$ as a 3D point cloud of constant probability density $|\psi|^2$. The orbital shape emerges from the product of the radial part $R_{nl}(r)$ and the angular part $Y_l^m(\theta,\varphi)$.
Full hydrogen wavefunction $$\psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\varphi) = \underbrace{\sqrt{\!\left(\frac{2}{na_0}\right)^{\!3}\frac{(n\!-\!l\!-\!1)!}{2n\,(n\!+\!l)!}}\; e^{-r/(na_0)}\!\left(\frac{2r}{na_0}\right)^{\!l} L_{n-l-1}^{2l+1}\!\!\left(\frac{2r}{na_0}\right)}_{R_{nl}(r)} \;\cdot\; Y_l^m(\theta,\varphi), \qquad E_n = -\frac{13.6\;\text{eV}}{n^2}$$

$a_0$ is the Bohr radius,  $L_p^q$ are associated Laguerre polynomials.
