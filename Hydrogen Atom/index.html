Visualise the hydrogen wavefunction $\psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\varphi)$ as a 3D point cloud of constant probability density $|\psi|^2$. The orbital shape emerges from the product of the radial part $R_{nl}(r)$ and the angular part $Y_l^m(\theta,\varphi)$.
Full hydrogen wavefunction $$\psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\varphi) = \underbrace{\frac{2}{n^2}\sqrt{\left(\frac{Z}{a_0}\right)^{\!3}\frac{(n\!-\!l\!-\!1)!}{(n\!+\!l)!}}\; \rho^{l}\,e^{-\rho/2} L_{n-\ell-1}^{(2\ell+1)}\!(\rho)}_{R_{n\ell}(r)} \;\cdot\; Y_\ell^m(\theta,\varphi), \qquad \rho = \frac{2Z}{n}\,\frac{r}{a_0}$$ $$E_n = -\frac{\mu Z^2 e^4}{2\hbar^2}\,\frac{1}{n^2} = -\frac{e^2}{2a_0}\,\frac{Z^2}{n^2} = -\frac{Z^2}{n^2}\,\text{Ry}$$

$a_0 = \hbar^2/(\mu e^2) \simeq 0.53\,\text{Å}$ is the Bohr radius,  $L_{n_r}^{(\nu)}(\rho)$ are the associated Laguerre polynomials,  $1\,\text{Ry} \simeq 13.6\,\text{eV}$.

Radial Probability Density 1D Plot
The function $u_{n\ell}(r)=r\,R_{n\ell}(r)$ satisfies a 1-D Schrödinger equation with an effective potential. Its square $|u_{n\ell}(r)|^2 = r^2|R_{n\ell}(r)|^2$ gives the probability of finding the electron between $r$ and $r+dr$, irrespective of direction.
Definition & normalisation $$u_{n\ell}(r) \;=\; r\,R_{n\ell}(r)$$ $$\int_0^{\infty}|u_{n\ell}(r)|^2\,dr = \int_0^{\infty}r^2|R_{n\ell}(r)|^2\,dr = 1$$

The peak positions give the most probable radii; the number of nodes equals $n-\ell-1$ (the radial quantum number $n_r$).
