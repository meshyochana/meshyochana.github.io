1. Laplace's Equation in Spherical Coordinates Theory
Separation of variables in spherical coordinates leads directly to the spherical harmonics as eigenfunctions of the angular part.
$$\nabla^2 f = \frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}\!\left(r^2\frac{\partial f}{\partial r}\right) + \frac{1}{r^2\sin\theta}\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\!\left(\sin\theta\frac{\partial f}{\partial\theta}\right) + \frac{1}{r^2\sin^2\theta}\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial\varphi^2} = 0$$

Consider solutions of the form $f(r,\theta,\varphi) = R(r)\,Y(\theta,\varphi)$. Substituting and separating gives one radial and one angular equation:

Radial equation $$\frac{1}{R}\frac{d}{dr}\!\left(r^2\frac{dR}{dr}\right) = \lambda$$

$\lambda = l(l+1)$, $l=0,1,2,\ldots$, for regularity at the origin.

Angular equation $$\frac{1}{Y\sin\theta}\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\!\left(\sin\theta\frac{\partial Y}{\partial\theta}\right) + \frac{1}{Y\sin^2\!\theta}\frac{\partial^2 Y}{\partial\varphi^2} = -\lambda$$

Setting $Y(\theta,\varphi)=\Theta(\theta)\Phi(\varphi)$ and separating again:

Azimuthal equation $$\frac{1}{\Phi}\frac{d^2\Phi}{d\varphi^2} = -m^2$$

Solution: $\Phi(\varphi)=e^{im\varphi}$, $\;m\in\mathbb{Z}$.

Polar (Legendre) equation $$\lambda\sin^2\!\theta + \frac{\sin\theta}{\Theta}\frac{d}{d\theta}\!\left(\sin\theta\frac{d\Theta}{d\theta}\right) = m^2$$

After $x=\cos\theta$: the associated Legendre ODE with solution $P_l^m(\cos\theta)$.

General solution — Spherical Harmonics: $$Y_l^m(\theta,\varphi) = \mathcal{N}_{lm}\,P_l^{|m|}(\cos\theta)\,e^{im\varphi}, \qquad \mathcal{N}_{lm} = \sqrt{\frac{2l+1}{4\pi}\frac{(l-|m|)!}{(l+|m|)!}}$$ The general solution to Laplace's equation regular at the origin is $$f(r,\theta,\varphi) = \sum_{l=0}^{\infty}\sum_{m=-l}^{l}\!\left(A_{lm}\,r^l + B_{lm}\,r^{-(l+1)}\right)Y_l^m(\theta,\varphi).$$

Orthonormality: $\displaystyle\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^{\pi} \bigl(Y_l^m\bigr)^* Y_{l'}^{m'}\sin\theta\,d\theta\,d\varphi = \delta_{ll'}\delta_{mm'}$  ·  Conjugate: $Y_l^{-m}=(-1)^m(Y_l^m)^*$

5. Hydrogen Atom — Full 3D Solution 3D Interactive
Visualise the hydrogen wavefunction $\psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\varphi)$ as a 3D isosurface of constant probability density $|\psi|^2$. The orbital shape emerges from the product of the radial part $R_{nl}(r)$ and the angular part $Y_l^m(\theta,\varphi)$.
Full hydrogen wavefunction $$\psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\varphi) = \underbrace{\sqrt{\!\left(\frac{2}{na_0}\right)^{\!3}\frac{(n\!-\!l\!-\!1)!}{2n\,(n\!+\!l)!}}\; e^{-r/(na_0)}\!\left(\frac{2r}{na_0}\right)^{\!l} L_{n-l-1}^{2l+1}\!\!\left(\frac{2r}{na_0}\right)}_{R_{nl}(r)} \;\cdot\; Y_l^m(\theta,\varphi), \qquad E_n = -\frac{13.6\;\text{eV}}{n^2}$$
