Calculation of allowed wavenumbers

Prepare the graphical representation

We are using the scaled units from class.
Use the slider below to set the depth $\hat{V_0}$ of the potential well.

$\hat{V_0}$ = 45
What this plot shows: The bound-state condition for a finite square well of half-width $a$ and depth $V_0$ requires that the inside wavenumber $k$ and the outside decay constant $\beta$ satisfy $\hat{k}^2 + \hat{\beta}^2 = \hat{V_0}$.
  • Red curve — Even states: $\hat{\beta} = \hat{k}\tan \hat{k}$ (boundary-condition equation for even/symmetric wavefunctions).
  • Green curve — Odd states: $\hat{\beta} = -\hat{k}\cot \hat{k}$ (boundary-condition equation for odd/antisymmetric wavefunctions).
  • Blue quarter-circle: $\hat{\beta} = \sqrt{\hat{V_0} - \hat{k}^2}$, the energy conservation constraint.
The intersection points of the quarter-circle with the red or green curves give the allowed wavenumbers $\hat{k_n}$ of the bound states. Each intersection corresponds to one bound state; the dots mark the numerically computed roots. Increasing $\hat{V_0}$, by dragging the slider, deepens the well while enlarging the blue quarter-circle, thus adding new intersections and increasing the number of bound states.
